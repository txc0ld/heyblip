# Executive Experience Summary

## What The App Felt Like Before

The app already looked more polished than a typical prototype, but several secondary surfaces still felt assembled rather than productized because they mixed live runtime state with sample content, private view models, or dead-end controls.

## What Was Wrong

- The strongest location-sharing surface still drifted away from real runtime state.
- Public and responder festival tools still overpromised capability.
- Purchase state risked splitting across sheets.
- Some action surfaces remained visually broader than the reliable behavior behind them.

## What Was Improved

- Nearby now leads more clearly into a live-state friend-finder experience.
- Full-screen friend finder is now oriented around shared runtime state, explicit availability, and calmer controls.
- Lost & Found and Medical are discoverable without pretending to be live.
- Store/paywall ownership is more coherent and outcome-oriented.
- SOS entry is better aligned to the shared responder flow model.

## How Functionality And Beauty Were Unified

The refinement strategy favored truthfulness over cosmetic expansion. Instead of adding more visual treatment, the branch removed unsupported controls, tightened hierarchy, reused shared state, and turned fake-success surfaces into deliberate readiness states. That produces a calmer and more credible interface.

## Remaining Weaknesses

- Real-device validation is still the largest experience gap for location and mesh-dependent behavior.
- Some festival utility workflows remain intentionally unavailable.
- Simulator-backed XCTest remains unstable in this environment.

## Final Judgment

Quality is materially higher than before, especially in trust-sensitive UX. The app feels more coherent and less misleading, but it is still not fully product-ready until location-sharing validation, responder workflows, and festival public-channel wiring are completed.
